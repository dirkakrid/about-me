Art of the Software Engineer

I'm a Full Stack Software Engineer from San Francisco with an interest in User Interface Design. I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, intuitive, minimalist interface designs. I attended San Jose State with a degree in Business Marketing, and a Software Engineering certificate from Hackbright Acadamy. I worked at FATHOM I worked at as Full Stack software Engineer specializing in the development of high-quality user interfaces delivering optimal user experiences across web, application, and other platforms. Collaborative communication among end users, designers, and developers while effectively creating well-received products. Extensive technical skills spanning Languages, frameworks and libraries, and applications and tools. Big picture thinker excelling in solution development for intricate problems. Prior to working at FATHOM I worked at Groupon as a product operational specialist, launching multiple core products and taking those products from pilot stage to full mainstream launch. I developed standard operating procedures and testing protocols to ensure high product quality for end users. In 2013, I attended the Grace Hopper conference, where my interest in programming took root. Now I am a full time software engineer with a passion for programming and design.

The Designer


I think of software engineering as an art; I like to combine creativity and logic into my software. I feel this gives me a unique perspective. I like to find inspiration in everyday things when it comes to programing and designing web applications. My inspiration comes from reading a book, walking the streets of San Francisco, or incorporating technology to solve my own problems. I often feel people forget how creative software development can be. Each artist has her own medium. Some use canvas, some paper, some paint on walls. My medium is my computer, where my creativity, inspiration, and imagination come to life. My paintbrush is my code.
